Work-related accidents kill nearly three million people each year and cost an estimated 4\% of global GDP, yet the CCTV networks already installed across industrial sites still depend on human observers whose sustained attention collapses within 20--35 minutes. This paper presents a monitoring pipeline that repurposes existing camera infrastructure at OCP Group phosphate-processing facilities for continuous personal protective equipment (PPE) compliance detection. A YOLOv8n detector (3.0M parameters) is fine-tuned at $640\times640$ resolution on a composite dataset that fuses the public Roboflow PPE-Detection corpus, SH17, and a dedicated respirator dataset covering the gas-mask class that construction-centric benchmarks omit; a site-specific OCP fine-tuning stage with weighted sampling is designed and in progress. Because a detector alone would shift the vigilance burden onto staff triaging false alarms, every flagged detection is re-examined by a Qwen2.5-VL judge served through Ollama, which reasons about occlusion, glare, distance, and lighting and returns a structured verdict with a stated reason before any alert is dispatched. After 50 epochs of training, the detector reaches \phX{} precision, \phX{} recall, and \phX{} mAP@50 at \phX{}~ms per frame; qualitative case studies illustrate the judge's verification behavior. The pipeline requires no new camera hardware, offering a retrofit path to fatigue-free safety monitoring.
